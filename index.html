Overview

While we have seen tremendous growth in large foundation models that have exhibited impressive scaling properties and emergent capabilities, we have yet to see such transformative improvements in robot learning. The current explosion in capable foundation models like GPT and CLIP has been driven in part by scaling large datasets. We are faced with several challenges: (1) Due to the high cost of collecting robot data, it is challenging to collect a sufficiently large robot dataset, (2) existing robot data is not conducive to current learning paradigms, and (3) there has been inadequate attention placed on elegantly integrating meta-data, which arise during data collection, into the training process.

This workshop advocates to search for novel modalities for scalable data collection beyond commonly utilized methodologies today, which center on teleoperated first-person robot demonstrations. The inaugural Workshop on Modalities for Scaling Real-world Data Collection aims to bring together an interdisciplinary cohort of robotics researchers and practitioners to brainstorm creative or unconventional modalities for robot learning.

Topics of interest include:

  • Scalable data collection strategies formats like play or reset-free data collection
  • Teleoperation / kinesthetic teaching / puppeteering
  • Intervention / preference / human feedback
  • Synthetic non-simulated data
  • Non-robot data (human first-person and third-person videos)
  • Cross-embodiment data
  • Scaling laws of robot data for quantity, quality, and utility
  • Human-centric interfaces like sketches, speech, or gestures
  • Hardware tools for low-cost wrist-mounted EEF data collection
  • Hand tracking or smartphone based teleoperation
  • Extracting more useful information from offline datasets (semantics, motion, goals)
